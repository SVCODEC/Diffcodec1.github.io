Diffcodec: Generative Codec Based on Diffusion Model for Speech Coding


Huaifeng Zhang 1, Yong Gao2, Guigeng Li1, Zhen Wang 1, Tingting Lv1*, Hao Zhang1*,

1College of Electronic Engineerin, Ocean University of China, QingDao, 266100, China

2College of Shipbuilding Engineering, Qingdao Innovation and Development Base of Harbin Engineering University, QingDao, 266000, China

Abstract.

Although generative adversarial network (GAN)-based speech codecs achieve considerable intelligibility and perceptual quality, the inherent discrete nature of the quantization process inevitably introduces imperceptible phase distortions and spectral gaps during the reconstruction of generated speech from discrete vectors. These distortions manifest as subtle waveform misalignments, hinder the improvement of objective metrics, and stem from an incomplete mapping from continuous coding vectors to discrete vectors. Inspired by the superior performance of diffusion models in speech enhancement, we innovatively reformulate GAN-based codecs as a diffusion-based generative codec, termed Diffcodec. This model employs a U-Net to model the data probability distribution from generated speech to original speech. Instead of forcing generated speech to gradually approximate original speech through feature constraints via discriminative adversarial learning, it learns the probability distribution from generated speech to original speech through a forward process and then recovers generated samples closest to the original speech via reverse denoising---innovatively transforming the adversarial training task into a generative task. To train multiple model variants, we also introduce a multi-stage training framework, which not only shortens training time but also improves output speech quality. This enables Diffcodec to provide a portable solution for generative tasks across different frameworks. Additionally, we introduce the concept of multi-resolution and apply it to speech reconstruction tasks, supplementing and improving spectral details at different scales, thereby significantly enhancing the output speech quality. For comprehensive comparative evaluation, we implement multiple mainstream GAN-based model variants and multiple Diffcodec variants. Comparative experiments demonstrate that Diffcodec exhibits absolute advantages over GAN-based codecs in various aspects such as training time, training stability, objective metrics, and spectral details, achieving a Perceptual Evaluation of Speech Quality (PESQ) of 3.59, an Extended Short-Time Objective Intelligibility (ESTOI) of 0.92, and a Virtual Speech Quality Objective Listener (ViSQOL) of 3.62. This confirms the innovation and superiority of applying diffusion models to the speech coding process.
Keywords: speech codec; generative adversarial network; schrödinger bridge; diffusion model; multi-resolution
